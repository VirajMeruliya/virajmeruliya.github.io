About Me

I’m a fourth-year PhD student in theoretical physics at McGill University, supervised by Prof. Alex Maloney. My research focuses on two-dimensional Conformal Field Theory and three-dimensional Gravity, with particular emphasis on non-perturbative effects and chaotic properties. I’m also deeply interested in data science and machine learning, particularly the application of physics-inspired methods to AI systems.

Research

My research uses analytical and numerical techniques to study non-perturbative effects in 3D gravity and CFTs, with applications to quantum information and AdS/CFT. Recently, I’ve been exploring bootstrap techniques and convex optimization for thermal quantum systems.

Data Science & ML

I'm currently exploring machine learning through projects like time-series prediction and classification with XGBoost, and attending Applied ML and NLP courses at McGill. I’ve participated in the Erdős Institute Bootcamp and mentored data science teams through full pipelines.

Projects

  • ML: Classification of brain activity using XGBoost and deep learning techniques.
  • Physics: Resurgence in Liouville Theory, black hole microstate counting using modular invariance.
