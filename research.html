Research

I completed my Ph.D. in Theoretical Physics at McGill University, where my research focused on exploring quantum gravity and conformal field theories, particularly in low-dimensional settings. My work combines advanced techniques from quantum field theory, holography, and resurgence theory.

Education

  • PhD in Physics, McGill University (2021-2025)
  • Bachelors and Masters of Science (Physics), IISER Pune (2019-2021)

Research Interests

  • Conformal Field Theory and its applications in quantum gravity
  • Gauge/Gravity duality and the AdS/CFT correspondence
  • Non-perturbative effects and resurgence in 2D and 3D quantum gravity
  • Black hole microstate counting and modular bootstrap techniques
  • Mathematical physics tools in string theory and low-dimensional models

Selected Publications & Presentations

  • Resurgence in Liouville Theory – Explored resurgence techniques in 2D gravity and their implications for path integrals in AdS3/CFT2
  • Statistics of 3D Black Holes from Liouville Line Defects – Investigated the modular properties of partition functions via conformal defects
  • Conformal Blocks and the Sum Over Geometries in Quantum Gravity – Presented at String Math and several departmental seminars
